Wales' Finest Castle: Conwy.

Perched majestically above the river, Conwy castle is a wonderful sight to behold, dominating the local landscape and forming an integral part of Edward I's Ring of Iron. With the mountans of Snowdonia behind, a Thomas Telford bridge infront and amazing town walls stretching out from within, it is hard to argue that this is one of the finest castles in Britain.

Clwydian Range: AONB.

Nestled in the far north east of Wales and forming an imposing barrier between England and Wales, the Clwyds are one of five Areas of Outstanding Natural Beauty in Wales. Read more about their beauty in this blog entry.

Wales: Land of the castle.

With more castles per square km than any another country on earth, Wales is the perfect place to explore the fortifications of times gone by. Whether its Norman castles, White castles, Welsh castles or Private castles, read this blog to find out all you need to know.

M4: Severn Bridges.
